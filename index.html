About

I am a Senior Applied Scientist at the GenAI Innovation Center at Amazon Web Services, where I work on making large language models more reliable, better calibrated, and grounded in principled foundations.

My research sits at the intersection of deep learning theory and practice. I develop information-theoretic and Bayesian approaches to understand and improve how AI systems reason, quantify uncertainty, and learn efficiently from limited supervision.

Before AWS, I was a Sr. Staff Research Scientist at Samsung SDS Research America and worked at Moody's Analytics. I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from Yale University and a B.S. in Mathematics from KAIST. I was a Simons Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Texas at Austin.

What I Work On

LLM Reasoning & Self-Evaluation

Methods for evaluating logical consistency, improving instruction-following, and enabling reliable self-assessment in large language models.

Uncertainty & Calibration

Principled Bayesian and information-theoretic approaches for quantifying uncertainty, calibrating predictions, and guiding efficient data acquisition.

Mathematical Foundations

Information theory, entropy inequalities, and combinatorics to establish rigorous mathematical foundations for learning and inference.

Selected Work

ICLR 2026 Workshop Best Paper Award

Quantifying Consistency in LLM Logical Reasoning via Structural Uncertainty

Proposes a structural uncertainty framework to measure logical consistency in LLM reasoning, offering a new lens on when and why language models fail at logic.

B Chaudhury, MF Wang, HH Park, R Ghosh, S Hong, JO Woo
EACL 2026 Findings

Reflect, Rewrite, Repeat: How Simple Arithmetic Enables Advanced Reasoning in Small Language Models

Shows that iterative self-reflection on simple arithmetic tasks can bootstrap advanced reasoning abilities in small language models without large-scale pretraining.

MF Wang, H Xie, MY Kim, B Chaudhury, M Ashok, S Gunturu, S Hong, ...
NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

Can LLMs Reliably Evaluate Themselves? A Probabilistic VC Framework

Applies VC-theoretic tools to rigorously characterize when and to what extent large language models can reliably self-evaluate.

JO Woo, MF Wang, R Ghosh, B Chaudhury, MY Kim
ICML 2024

Improving Instruction Following in Language Models through Proxy-Based Uncertainty Estimation

Connects uncertainty estimation to instruction following in LLMs, using proxy-based methods to improve reliability without retraining the base model.

J Lee, JO Woo, J Seok, P Hassanzadeh, W Jang
ICLR 2023 Top 25%

Active Learning in Bayesian Neural Networks with Balanced Entropy Learning Principle

Introduces a new principle for leveraging uncertainty in active learning, enabling more balanced and effective data acquisition through Bayesian neural networks.

JO Woo
IEEE ISIT 2022

Analytic Mutual Information in Bayesian Neural Networks

Derives a closed-form expression for mutual information in Bayesian neural networks, bridging information-theoretic foundations with practical deep learning.

JO Woo
