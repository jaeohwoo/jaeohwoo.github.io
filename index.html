About

I work at the GenAI Innovation Center at Amazon Web Services, focusing on making large language models more reliable, better calibrated, and grounded in principled mathematical foundations.

My research connects deep learning theory with practice — developing information-theoretic and Bayesian methods to understand how AI systems reason, handle uncertainty, and learn efficiently from limited data.

Previously I was a Sr. Staff Research Scientist at Samsung SDS Research America and worked at Moody’s Analytics. I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from Yale University and a B.S. from KAIST. I was a Simons Postdoctoral Fellow at UT Austin.

Research Areas

LLM Reasoning & Self-Evaluation

Methods for evaluating logical consistency, improving instruction-following, and enabling reliable self-assessment in large language models.

Uncertainty & Calibration

Principled Bayesian and information-theoretic approaches for quantifying uncertainty, calibrating predictions, and guiding efficient data acquisition.

Mathematical Foundations of ML

Entropy inequalities, information theory, and combinatorics to establish rigorous mathematical foundations for learning and inference.
